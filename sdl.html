Self-driving Lab (SDL)

During my time at Georgia Tech in the Active Materials and Additive Manufacturing laboratory, led by Dr. Jerry Qi, my thesis project was: "Self-driving lab of photopolymer synthesis and mechanical testing for expedited materials discovery." This work developed three key components:

  • Preparation of photopolymer inks using a robotic arm, closed-loop liquid dispensing system for monomers/crosslinkers, and powder dispenser for photoinitiators.
  • Curing photopolymer inks with a custom rheological sensor to determine time, temperature, and realization of the photopolymerization process.
  • Automated tensile testing on tensile bars using a robotic arm, force sensor, and automated clamp, with cameras for anomaly detection via machine learning and digital image correlation for sample dimensions.

Automated data processing and property extraction calculated material properties curing time, curing temperature, realizability, modulus of elasticity, elongation, and ultimate tensile strength. Throughout the project, there was an active collaboration with Mr. Ayush Jain who maintained the active learning algorithm and database.
