Design Gallery

QUbeSat Hardware

Bespoke CubeSat hardware with optimized geometry for additive manufacturing. See QUbeSat project page for more details.

Custom Robot Gripper

Custom robot gripper designed to pick and place parts in the material composition system of the self-driving lab. The gripper uses a servo motor to manipulate the gripper fingers and is programmed through an Arduino. See the self-driving lab project page for a video demonstration.

Mass Flow Plug (MFP) Rake 1

MFP rakes designed to measure the output of scramjet engines durign testing. Water cooling channels integrated to withstand high temperatures. 3D printed out of copper because of complex geometries and thermal conductivity.

MFP Rake 2

Alternative MFP rake with more complex water and air flow channels. In addition, a thermocouple is integrated to measure the temperature during testing.

Curing Time System Testing Platform

The curing time system platform is designed to test the curing properties of photopolymer inks as a part of the self-driving lab. Springs compress the two hands to secure a container of photopolymer ink. There are also integrated UV LEDs which are used to cure the ink. See the self-driving lab project page for more details and a video demonstration.

Automated Tensile Testing Gripper

The automated tensile testing gripper is designed to secure the bottom half of the tensile bars during testing. It uses a stepper motor and lead screw to linearly move the grips. See the self-driving lab project page for more details and a video demonstration.

Powder Dispenser

The powder dispenser is designed to dispense photoinitiators into photopolymer inks during the processing portion of the self-driving lab. The system uses a stepper motor to turn gears that allow powder to be dispensed. The speed of the motor and the number of revolutions determine the amount of powder dispensed. See the self-driving lab project page for more details.
